About

I've always been drawn to making things — figuring out how they work, taking them apart, building them back up. That instinct has threaded through everything: years in sales, time in the classroom, and countless hours hands-on with aircraft and payloads.

Teaching turned out to be one of the best engineering educations I ever got. There's nothing like having to explain a system to a room full of students to find out how well you actually understand it.

These days the focus is unmanned systems — making them smarter, more capable, and more useful. I believe in open-source development, shared knowledge, and the idea that the best work comes from people who aren't afraid to get their hands dirty.

And sometimes cause a little trouble in the process.

Builds & Projects

360° GoPro Rig

I saw early how big 360° video was going to be, so before Insta360 and its peers made it easy, I built my own rigs — first a cube of GoPros, then a hexagon for more resolution, each camera recording its own stream to be stitched into one video later. It started as a handheld rig, but every shot came out with the back of my head in it, so I built a wheeled dolly and then a robotically controlled one to get myself out of frame. More on my YouTube channel — youtube.com/@travisclaypool.

Truck-Mounted Projection Screen

A guerrilla marketing build: a pop-up projection screen viewable from both sides, plus the frame to mount it on a truck bed. A generator, a computer, and a short-throw projector drove the whole thing; the screen itself was a translucent shower curtain. It worked.

The Connected Cactus

I designed and taught a TechShop class called "The Connected Cactus." Students walked in knowing nothing about plants or electronics and walked out with a cactus that tweeted whenever it got thirsty — an ESP8266 and a soil-moisture sensor doing the talking. Taught back before TechShop closed for good.

The Glowing Dictionary

A hollowed-out dictionary that reveals an infinity mirror when opened — lighting up on open, going dark on close, with a new light pattern each time. Battery-powered and USB-rechargeable. It turned out so well that I put it on display at an event, and someone stole it.

Live-Edge Coffee Table

A live-edge slab on hairpin legs — one of several carpentry detours, before resin river tables caught my interest to explore.

Branded Bread

I baked the loaf and I baked the cookie — I like every part of making, cooking included. Add a laser engraver to the mix, and custom-toasted graphics were a natural outcome.

Team Spirit Metalwork

Waterjet-cut college logos, welded to horseshoes and rebar, then powder-coated — made for an FFA charity auction. They're drink holders, specifically so your drink has somewhere to sit while you're throwing horseshoes.

Dual Extruder Upgrade

A customer's single-extruder printer, upgraded to dual for multi-material prints. Designed the new mounts in Fusion 360 around a BondTech extruder and E3D hotend, printed the parts, and got it dialed in.

Carved Pumpkins

A buddy and I have been carving pumpkins on the laser engraver for over a decade. We try to hand them out before Thanksgiving — laser-etched, they hold up clean through October and Halloween — and people genuinely love getting one.

Ducted Fan

3D-printed drone fan ducts and a ducted fan housing with a five-bladed impeller — designed as printable assemblies for UAV propulsion.

Tiny Forklift

A palm-sized 3D-printed forklift — forks, cabin, wheels and all. It did not stay singular. Somewhere past a thousand prints, I started bagging them by color and tallying boxes by the hundred.

Touch-Reactive LED Wall Installation

Conductive paint and copper tape traced across the wall as circuit-board artwork — touch anywhere along a trace and the logo lit up in LEDs. Custom PCBs built around an Adafruit MPR121 capacitive touch sensor drove the whole thing.

Walk / Don't Walk Signs

I bid on an auction for a couple of pedestrian crossing signals and it turned out to be a pallet of about forty. I wired up a bunch so you could plug them in and flip a switch to toggle between walk and don't walk.

Aransas Pass Screen Printing

A parody of the Aransas Pass water tower — the real one has a fish, mine has a mosquito. Anyone who's spent a summer down there knows why. Made for friends and family.

Laser-Cut Mask Patterns

Cut fabric mask blanks on my laser cutter in the early days of the pandemic, bundled them up, and handed them off to a team of church folks who sewed them into finished masks.

Micro FPV Quadcopter

91 grams of EMAX motors, a runcam, and questionable judgment. Small enough to fly indoors, fast enough to regret it.

Geophysical Payload Integration

I get to work with cool people and cool drones — integrating survey payloads across all kinds of airframes.

Halloween Cardboard Builds

A cardboard megabox with three lives. It arrived holding a hot water heater, then got skinned into a wearable personal-space bubble at the peak of the pandemic — climb inside, head through the hole on top, and use the sticks tipped with tiny yellow virus balls to keep everyone six feet away. Finally it became a Magic School Bus–style rocket, complete with tinsel-lined nozzles that blew actual bubbles. Also in the frame: a Kraft Mac & Cheese costume built for a friend.

Cargo Datalogger

I was asked to build a data logger that could record accelerometer, gyro, and GPS data for hours on a single charge and save everything to an SD card. Built around an Arduino Mega and a u-blox M8N GPS module, with an MPU-9250 IMU for motion — all in a weatherproof enclosure built for a cargo payload.
